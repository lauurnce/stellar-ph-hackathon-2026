# Payment Platform Comparison for Freelancers

| Platform | Buyer Protection | Seller Protection | The Critical Gap for Freelancers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PayPal** | **Strong:** Full refund if item is not received or "not as described" (180-day window). | **Weak:** Covers unauthorized claims but **excludes digital/intangible services**. Requires physical tracking numbers. | **High Risk:** Clients can claim a digital service wasn't delivered. Since you can't provide a shipping tracking number, PayPal automatically sides with the buyer. You lose the money, the work, and pay a ₱900 fee. |
| **Credit Cards (Stripe)** | **Strong:** Bank-backed chargebacks (60–120 days) for "service not received" or "unauthorized." | **Moderate:** Can submit contracts/chat logs as evidence, but banks side with cardholders ~73% of the time. | **Access & Bias:** Stripe isn't fully supported for local PH marketplace setups. Furthermore, banks view digital screenshots as weak evidence compared to physical delivery receipts. |
| **Etsy** | **Strong:** Purchase Protection Program covers up to $250 for issues. | **Weak:** Only protects physical goods with shipping proof. Capped at $250. | **Wrong Model:** Built for products/templates, not services. High-value projects (e.g., $1,500 branding) have zero protection, and accounts can be frozen for 180 days during disputes. |
| **Vgen** | **Good:** Platform holds funds and refunds buyer if the seller fails to deliver. | **Good (for simple art):** Guaranteed payout if terms are met, but fees are high (9.4% – 10.4%). | **No Milestones:** Works only for one-off assets. Terrible for ongoing work or large projects because it lacks milestone-based escrow (it's either 100% upfront or 100% on delivery). |
| **RaketPH** | **None:** No documented escrow or dispute resolution system. | **None:** Payments are handled offline via GCash or bank transfers. | **Zero Security:** It functions as a job directory, not a payment guardian. If a client ghosts you after delivery, you have no platform recourse. |
